# Craft — what makes a piece good, not just moving

## The first rule: the visual comes from the concept — never default to one device

The motion vocabulary must be derived from *this idea*. **Reusing one device — "a thing grows up from the ground" — across unrelated concepts is the #1 way these go stale.** If you reach for "something grows" a second time, stop and ask what the concept actually looks like.

| Concept | Native visual (✓) | Lazy default (✗) |
|---|---|---|
| Prompting / iteration | a garden: prompts fall, most wither, one blooms | (here a plant is right — the concept is botanical) |
| **Compounding** | a dot **doubling** until it floods the screen + a live counter | a tree growing |
| **The blank page / cursor** | a lonely blinking cursor → first word → text **floods** the page | a sprout growing from the word |
| Overload / focus | tabs/windows multiplying into noise, then all but one close | — |

## Rhythm: resolve → a beat of stillness → reflect

The closing line is a *looking-back* statement; it only lands after the action stops. Let the motion finish, hold a beat, **then** reveal the slogan. Never overlap the conclusion with things still moving (it reads as "the story isn't over yet").

## Restraint

Motion needs fewer elements than static design — too many and it's noisy in motion. Keep only the core. (流白: "只留核心要素，避免用力过猛.")

## Causality

Tie cause → effect literally: an input lands → something happens *at that spot*. Loosely-related parallel events read as random noise. (Prompt Garden: each falling prompt lands and grows a plant at that exact point.)

## Spotlight the hero

When one element matters, dim the rest and lift it — a halo, a scale, a color shift.

## Easing

Never linear on anything a viewer reads. Ease the *value*, not just opacity. Match the curve to the idea: ease-in (slow→explosive) for compounding; a count-up settling for a number; overshoot for a small pop.

## Semantics — don't self-sabotage

If the piece depicts failure/withering, do **not** label it with your own product's features (it reads as "our product fails at its core jobs"). Use neutral or process language. (We changed falling "prompts" from product feature names — "logo reveal", "bar chart" — to iteration words — "warmer", "again", "almost" — and made the one that blooms say "yes.")

## Text content

Plain, concrete, lowercase-casual or quiet-serif depending on tone. The line should feel earned, not slapped on. Two-beat structure works well: a setup during the action, a payoff after it resolves.
