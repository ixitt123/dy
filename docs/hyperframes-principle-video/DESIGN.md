## Style Prompt

A technical explainer about HyperFrames as a video engine where HTML is the source, a deterministic timeline synchronizes motion, and the renderer captures frames into MP4. Use a deep ink canvas with porcelain text, copper signal accents, and green status details. The feel is precise, calm, and engineered, like a clean control-room diagram rather than a neon dashboard.

## Colors

- Background: `#10131a`
- Panel: `#191f2b`
- Foreground: `#f1eadc`
- Muted text: `#b8ad99`
- Accent: `#d89652`
- Status: `#83c99f`

## Typography

- Display and Chinese headings: `"Microsoft YaHei UI", "Microsoft YaHei", sans-serif`
- Data/code labels: `"IBM Plex Mono", "Cascadia Mono", monospace`

## Motion Rules

- Medium-energy explainer pacing with 0.35-0.55s entrances.
- Use push and crossfade transitions; keep outgoing scene content visible until transition begins.
- Ambient motion must be finite and subtle: breathing grid, signal dots, timeline pulse.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use purple-blue neon gradients.
- Do not use generic black, white, Inter, or Roboto defaults.
- Do not use jump cuts between scenes.
- Do not create random or time-based animation.
