## Style Prompt

A tense Chinese education explainer about parents misjudging how close the Zhongkao really is. Use an Ian-style white canvas with black hand-drawn structure lines, short red/orange/blue handwritten annotations, and an original Xiaohei 2.0 protagonist: a matte black 3D animated collectible character with soft vinyl texture, rounded proportions, expressive glossy eyes, short limbs, and a serious but slightly mischievous working attitude. The video should feel like a clean product sketch that suddenly becomes urgent, not a PPT lesson page.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#ffffff`
- Ink: `#111111`
- Soft ink: `#3a3a3a`
- Muted line: `#d8d2c7`
- Red warning: `#d9362b`
- Orange path: `#b76512`
- Blue system note: `#1f559e`

## Typography

- Chinese display and body: `"LXGW WenKai", "KaiTi", "Microsoft YaHei UI", "Microsoft YaHei", sans-serif`
- Small labels and counters: `"Cascadia Mono", "IBM Plex Mono", monospace`

## Motion Rules

- Medium-high urgency, with a strong hook in the first 3 seconds.
- Primary transition: horizontal push, like a whiteboard sheet being shoved forward.
- Accent transition: squeeze for the split-flow scene, as if choices are being compressed through a gate.
- Every scene has entrance animation; old scenes stay visible until the transition begins.
- One hero VFX per scene: red exam stamp, orange folding calendar path, black sand countdown, blue split-note particles, orange route line.
- Ambient motion is finite and deterministic.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use specific score lines, school admission data, or invented split percentages.
- Do not copy existing branded characters or named animation studio styles.
- Do not make the protagonist passive, flat, or decorative.
- Do not use PPT cards, formal flowcharts, dense UI, gradients behind the whole scene, or long Chinese explanations.
- Do not scare with false guarantees; keep the pressure based on the user's supplied copy.
