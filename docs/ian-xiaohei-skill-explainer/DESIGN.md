## Style Prompt

An Ian Xiaohei hand-drawn Chinese explainer for the `ian-xiaohei-illustrations` Codex skill. The video feels like a clean whiteboard sketch made by a product-minded AI worker: pure white canvas, black slightly uneven lines, a serious black Xiaohei character doing the core work, and restrained red, orange, and blue handwritten annotations. The look is clear, strange, sparse, and useful, never a PPT diagram or commercial cartoon.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#ffffff`
- Ink: `#111111`
- Soft ink: `#3a3a3a`
- Muted line: `#d6d1c9`
- Red annotation: `#e34234`
- Orange path: `#b76512`
- Blue system note: `#2f6fbd`

## Typography

- Chinese display and body: `"LXGW WenKai", "KaiTi", "Microsoft YaHei UI", "Microsoft YaHei", sans-serif`
- Small system labels: `"Cascadia Mono", "IBM Plex Mono", monospace`

## Motion Rules

- 16:9 landscape, 1920 by 1080.
- Medium-energy teaching pace with a strong hook in the first three seconds.
- Every scene uses entrance animation; outgoing scenes remain complete until the scene transition begins.
- Primary transition: push slide, like a sketch page being moved across the desk.
- Accent transition: squeeze on the QA scene, as if a messy draft is being compressed into one clean rule.
- Use one hero VFX behavior per scene: ink dust, red correction marks, blue signal notes, orange flow, paper scraps, or a settling final mark.
- All ambient loops are finite and deterministic.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use gradients, paper texture, shadows, stock-like illustration, or dark tech UI.
- Do not create PPT cards, formal flowcharts, dense architecture diagrams, or many equal boxes.
- Do not make Xiaohei cute, mascot-like, glossy, or decorative.
- Do not over-explain with long Chinese labels.
- Do not introduce extra brand colors beyond black, red, orange, and blue.
